Cell Biology — Core Notes

The cell is the basic structural and functional unit of life. Cells are of two
broad types: prokaryotic (no membrane-bound nucleus, e.g. bacteria) and
eukaryotic (membrane-bound nucleus and organelles, e.g. plant and animal cells).

Key organelles:
- Nucleus: stores DNA and controls cell activities.
- Mitochondria: the "powerhouse"; produces ATP via cellular respiration.
- Ribosomes: sites of protein synthesis.
- Endoplasmic reticulum (rough = with ribosomes, smooth = lipid synthesis).
- Golgi apparatus: modifies, sorts, and packages proteins.
- Chloroplast (plants only): carries out photosynthesis.
- Cell membrane: selectively permeable barrier controlling transport.

Photosynthesis (in chloroplasts):
  6CO2 + 6H2O + light energy -> C6H12O6 + 6O2

Cellular respiration (in mitochondria):
  C6H12O6 + 6O2 -> 6CO2 + 6H2O + ATP

Common exam traps:
1. Confusing prokaryotic vs eukaryotic (bacteria have NO nucleus).
2. Thinking only animal cells have mitochondria — plant cells have them too.
3. Mixing up photosynthesis (builds glucose) with respiration (breaks it down).
